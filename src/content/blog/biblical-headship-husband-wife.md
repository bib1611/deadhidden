# Biblical Headship Explained: What God Actually Requires of Husbands and Wives

The word "headship" makes people flinch.

That's intentional.

Our culture has decided that leadership in marriage is oppressive. That hierarchy is evil. That the only legitimate marriage is one where both partners hold equal power in every domain.

So when you mention biblical headship, people hear domination. Control. A man lording his authority over a woman who resents him.

That's not what the Scripture teaches. But that's what people hear because the truth has been buried.

Let me excavate it.

## What Ephesians 5:22-33 Actually Says

The passage is in your Bible. You've probably never read it straight.

Ephesians 5:22-24 — "Wives, submit yourselves unto your own husbands, as unto the Lord. For the husband is the head of the wife, even as Christ is the head of the church: and he is the saviour of the body. Therefore as the church is subject unto Christ, so let the wives be to your own husbands in every thing."

Then comes the husband's part.

Ephesians 5:25-31 — "Husbands, love your wives, even as Christ also loved the church, and gave himself for it; That he might sanctify and cleanse it with the washing of water by the word, That he might present it to himself a glorious church, not having spot, or wrinkle, or any such thing; but that it should be holy and without blemish. So ought men to love their own wives as their own bodies: he that loveth his wife loveth himself. For no man ever yet hated his own flesh; but nourisheth and cherisheth it, even as the Lord the church: For we are members of his body, of his flesh, and of his bones."

This passage is not what the modern church preaches.

The wife's call is submission. Not begrudging obedience. Submission — a willing alignment with her husband's leadership.

The husband's call is infinitely harder.

He must love his wife *as Christ loved the church*. How did Christ love the church? He *died for it*. He sanctified it. He cleaned it. He made it holy and without blemish.

That's the standard. Not "love her when you feel like it." Not "love her unless she disagrees with you." Love her like your own body. Nourish her. Cherish her. Sacrifice for her.

Notice what the passage does NOT say:

It doesn't say the husband gets to do whatever he wants.

It doesn't say the husband is always right.

It doesn't say the wife should abandon her mind or judgment.

It says the husband is the head. And headship means responsibility.

## Headship Is Responsibility, Not Privilege

Here's what the modern church — both conservative and progressive — gets wrong.

Conservatives think headship means authority. Authority means the man decides, and the woman complies. Simple.

Wrong.

Progressives think headship is oppressive. It's a relic of patriarchal oppression that must be dismantled.

Also wrong, but for different reasons.

Headship is leadership. Leadership is responsibility.

Think about it biologically. The head is connected to the entire body. The head receives input from the body. Eyes, ears, nose, touch. The head processes all of this information. Then the head directs the body.

But here's the part we miss: If the body is injured, the head feels it. If the body is in pain, the head experiences that pain.

Headship is not detached rule. It's intimate connection with accountability.

When the husband is the head, he bears responsibility for the family's spiritual condition. For the wife's growth toward holiness. For the children's formation. For the household's character.

That's a burden.

The husband who understands biblical headship doesn't wake up thinking, "What can I get my wife to do today?"

He wakes up thinking, "How can I lead my wife toward Christ? How can I protect my family? How can I provide for them? How can I teach my children God's truth?"

That's responsibility.

## Why a Wife Submits to Headship

The modern woman is told: Submission is weakness. Submission is oppression. A strong woman submits to no one.

This is a lie told by those who don't understand power.

Strength is not the refusal to yield. Strength is the ability to yield to something greater than yourself.

A woman who submits to her husband's biblical headship is not weak. She's displaying actual strength. She's saying: "My husband loves me sacrificially. He leads toward Christ. I trust him. I align myself with his leadership."

That's not weakness. That's faith.

This requires courage. It requires trust. It requires a husband who is actually worthy of that trust.

Here's the thing: A wife cannot submit to a husband who doesn't lead. She can only bury her frustration. A wife submits to a man who knows what he believes, who leads toward holiness, who sacrifices for her good.

That's the design.

And it works.

When a wife trusts her husband's leadership, something happens. Peace. Stability. A shared vision. Instead of two people pulling in different directions, there's alignment.

That alignment produces fruit.

Children sense it. They see a father who leads. A mother who supports that leadership. That foundation shapes everything downstream.

## Submission Is Strength, Not Weakness

I need to say this directly to women because the culture has lied to you.

Your strength is not in refusing to follow. Your strength is in choosing wisely where you yield.

A woman who submits to God is strong. A woman who submits to her husband is exercising the same strength — trusting a leader who himself submits to God.

This is the architecture of the home.

God leads Christ. Christ leads the church. The husband leads the wife. The wife leads the children (through support of the husband's authority). The children obey the parents.

It's not a pyramid of oppression. It's a structure that works.

Here's what Christie (my wife) understands that most women don't: Submission doesn't mean silence. It means alignment.

She has a voice. She has wisdom. She has perspective I lack. I need her counsel. I seek her input. And then I lead.

Sometimes she disagrees with my decision. Sometimes I'm wrong. But the structure remains: I lead, she supports that leadership while offering her perspective.

And you know what? Over 24 years of marriage, that structure has produced stability, faith, and partnership.

Not because she's weak. Because she's strong enough to submit.

And not because I'm a tyrant. Because I love her like Christ loved the church.

## What Biblical Headship Looks Like Monday Through Saturday

This is where it gets practical.

Headship isn't something the husband exercises once a week in a church pew. It's lived daily.

**Spiritual Leadership**

The husband reads Scripture. Not for himself only. For his family.

He prays. He leads prayer. He sets the spiritual tone of the home.

He makes decisions based on God's Word, not his preference. He says no to things that contradict Scripture. He says yes to things that build faith.

His children watch him. They see if he believes what he says he believes.

**Economic Provision**

The husband works. Not begrudgingly. As a calling.

He provides the primary income. He makes sure the bills are paid. He builds wealth for his family's future.

His wife can work. She can pursue career. But she's not burdened with being the economic anchor.

That's his job.

**Protection and Defense**

The husband shields his family from danger.

Physically: He's the one who gets up when there's a noise in the night. He's the protector.

Spiritually: He guards against false doctrine. He teaches his children truth. He defends his wife against slander.

Legally: He understands contracts, property law, financial structures. He handles these things.

Emotionally: He's steady. He doesn't rage. He doesn't abandon in crisis.

**Fathering and Training**

The husband fathers his children.

He teaches them. He disciplines them. He models what it means to fear God.

He's not absent. Not checked out. He's present. Daily.

His daughter learns what to expect from a man by watching her father. His son learns how to be a man by imitating his father.

**Leading in Conflict**

When marriage conflict happens (and it will), the husband leads through it.

He doesn't run. Doesn't shut down. Doesn't rage.

He listens to his wife. He seeks to understand her perspective. And then he decides how the family moves forward.

Sometimes that decision includes changing his mind. Sometimes it means holding firm on principle.

But he decides. And his wife, having been heard, aligns with that decision.

This requires humility. It requires strength. It requires a man who can receive input without becoming defensive.

## The Counterfeit: Egalitarian Marriage Repackaged

The modern church has a problem.

It knows biblical headship exists. The text is too clear to deny.

So it repackages it.

"Headship" becomes "servant leadership." "The husband should lead, but only if the wife agrees." "Submission means mutual submission." "It's a partnership of equals in every domain."

These are comfortable lies.

Headship is not a partnership of equals. A partnership has two heads. A marriage has one.

The wife has value. The wife has wisdom. The wife matters. But the structure is clear: The husband is the head.

Servants don't make that call. Equals don't make that call. The head does.

The repackaging sounds good. It feels modern. It appeals to our desire to avoid hard truths.

But it doesn't work.

Marriages built on "mutual submission" and "partnership of equals" collapse under pressure. When conflict comes, who decides? If both partners have equal say, the decision paralyzed by democracy.

So one of two things happens:

The wife becomes the de facto leader. The husband defaults. The wife resents carrying a burden. The marriage becomes hostile.

Or they reach an impasse. Nobody leads. The family drifts. There's no vision. No direction. Just two people hoping they agree.

That's not a marriage. That's a negotiation.

Biblical headship acknowledges reality: Two people can't have equal authority in every domain. Someone has to lead.

The Scripture assigns that role to the husband. And the blessing comes from accepting that assignment.

## How to Build This in Your Marriage

If you're married, you're not in this structure yet. Not fully.

That's okay. Most marriages aren't.

But you can start moving toward it.

**For Husbands**

First: Repent of abdicating. If you've let your wife lead, if you've deferred all decisions, if you've been passive — own it. Repent.

Then: Start leading. Small things first. Decide on dinner without asking. Decide on the weekend. Make a family financial decision. Lead in prayer.

Your wife might resist. She's been carrying weight you should carry. She won't trust the transition immediately.

Be consistent. Show her you're trustworthy. Lead toward her good, not your preference.

This doesn't mean dictatorship. It means deciding, communicating clearly, and asking for her input — but you retain the authority to decide.

Over time, she'll align. Not out of fear. Out of trust built through demonstrated leadership.

**For Wives**

First: Trust. Not blind trust. Trust that your husband is seeking God.

If he's not leading, invite him to lead. "I'd like you to make the call on this." Give him space.

If he resists, pray. Encourage him. Show him you want his leadership.

But don't take over. Don't micromanage. Don't show contempt when he leads differently than you would.

Align yourself with his leadership. It's an act of faith.

Remember: submission doesn't mean stupidity. You bring your wisdom, your perspective, your voice. And then you trust his leadership. That's the structure that works.

**For Both**

Study Ephesians 5 together. Not with commentary. Just read it. What does it actually say?

The husband loves sacrificially. The wife submits respectfully. That's the call.

Pray together. Confess where you've failed. Ask God to reshape your marriage toward His design.

It takes time. It requires humility. It demands that you trust God more than your culture.

But it works. The marriages that survive crisis, that produce children with conviction, that outlast every trend — they're built on this structure.

This isn't theory. This is design proven over 2000 years of Church history and countless faithful marriages.

Build on it.

## The Action Challenge

This week:

**Husbands:** Make one decision that belongs in your domain and lead it. Don't ask permission. Don't debate. Decide and communicate with love.

**Wives:** In one area, step back and let your husband lead. Don't correct. Don't manage. Support his leadership.

Both: Read Ephesians 5 together. Talk about what it means in your marriage.

That's the start.
